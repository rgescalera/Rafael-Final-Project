Immediately hazardous housing code violations plague five community districts across Brooklyn and the Bronx

Violations such as leaky roofs, faulty appliances, and mold are much more common in neighborhoods such as Bushwick and East Flatbush

By Rafael Escalera Montoto

December 7, 2023


New York’s long history is catching up to itself, more quickly in some community districts (CD) than in others.

Five community districts in The Bronx and Brooklyn account for almost 25% of all immediately hazardous housing code violations across New York City, according to the Association for Neighborhood and Housing Development’s latest Housing Risk Chart. The remaining 75% of those violations are distributed across the other 54 community districts in the city. One explanation for the high concentration in these five communities could be their long history of property ownership, combined with many of the original homeowners’ lack of resources to provide upkeep, says Celestina León, the District Manager for Bushwick, one of the top five districts. Minority populations are especially impacted. In bushwick, the population is over 50% hispanic and almost 20% Black.
